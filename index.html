About

Hei! My name is Paolo.
I was born in Trieste (TS), a small city in the North-East of Italy, where I attended my Primary, Middle and High school. I then moved to Torino (TO) where I got my Bachelor (Telecommunications Engineering) and Master Degree (Mechatronic Engineering), both at the Politecnico di Torino.
I'm currently in love with drones and robotics. In 2017, while studying, I worked on the design and implementation algorithms of an autonomous UAV for a local company. After that, I succesfully completed the Master ICT and Advanced Projecting (2 years) at the University of Torino, to improve my skills. In my free time I study electric guitar, sometimes I write songs. I also do a lot of physical activities.
I am currently working with Dr. Kostas Alexis at the Autonomous Robots Lab (ARL) . After one year spent at the University of Nevada, Reno (USA), where I got my second Master (Computer Science), I moved to Trondheim (NO), as a PhD candidate at NTNU.

Collision-tolerant Navigation in Confined Environments

This work presents the design and autonomous navigation policy of the Resilient Micro Flyer, a new type of collision-tolerant robot tailored to flying through extremely confined environments and manhole-sized tubes. The robot maintains a low weight (<500g) and implements a combined rigid-compliant design through the integration of elastic flaps around its stiff collision-tolerant frame.

ARS (Autonomous Remote Sensing)

The main goal of the ARS (Autonomous Remote Sensing) project was to design and prototype a drone capable of carrying out autonomous inspection of confined spaces, in particular of tunnels. Exploiting the concept of Data Muling, the UAV was able to navigate inside a GPS-denied environment without any help from the operator, collecting data from pre-disposed BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy) modules and, in post-processing, create a 3D model of the inspected environment with an average error of ~1mm.

Autonomous Indoor UAV Navigation & Mapping

A project for indoor, unknown, GPS-denied environment navigation of UAVs. The main used sensors are a 2D Lidar laser scanner range finder and a Pixhawk flight controller. All computation are performed onboard.
